🔬 The Process

How FitGuide works

From your workout description to a full sports science report — here's exactly what happens.

1

Describe your routine

Type your workout in plain language — exercises, sets, reps, how many days per week, your split, and your goals. You don't need to be precise; even a partial description works. The more detail you give, the more accurate your analysis will be.

2

AI-powered sports science analysis

FitGuide's AI — trained on exercise science principles — reads your routine and evaluates the training variables: exercise selection, volume, intensity, frequency, and mechanical load. It cross-references this with established physiological adaptation pathways.

3

Your archetype is identified

Based on your training profile, you're matched to a specific athletic archetype — like "The Explosive Athlete" or "The Hypertrophy Specialist." This tells you exactly what kind of athlete your current program is conditioning you to become.

4

Receive your performance timeline

You'll see a week-by-week breakdown of what physiological changes are expected — from early neurological adaptations in weeks 1–2, to measurable structural changes at month 4+. Projections are specific and clinical, not generic.

5

Understand your physical adaptations

Get a list of specific, measurable performance metrics you can expect to improve — like vertical jump height in centimetres, sprint time in seconds, or maximal strength benchmarks — all tied to the mechanism behind the adaptation.

The Science

What we analyse

FitGuide evaluates the key training variables that determine your physiological outcomes.

📐

Volume & Intensity

Total training volume (sets × reps × load) and relative intensity determine whether your training drives hypertrophy, strength, or power adaptations.

🔁

Training Frequency

How often you train each muscle group or movement pattern directly impacts the rate and type of physiological adaptation you can achieve per week.

⚡

Exercise Selection

Whether you're doing plyometrics, Olympic lifts, or machine-based isolation determines the neuromuscular demand and specificity of your adaptation.

🧬

Physiological Adaptation Pathways

Different training stimuli activate different adaptation mechanisms — from fast-twitch fibre recruitment to tendon stiffness changes and mitochondrial density.
